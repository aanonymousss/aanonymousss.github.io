FACTSPEECH: SPEAKING A FOREIGN LANGUAGE PRONUNCIATION USING ONLY YOUR NATIVE CHARACTERS

Anonymous

ABSTRACT

Recent Text-to-Speech models have been requested to synthesize natural speech from code-mixed sentences because they are commonly used in real-world applications. While a lot of studies strive to synthesize natural speech from a code-mixed sentence, most of them cannot generate fluent native speech from code-mixed text, especially in transliterated words. In this paper, we present FACTSpeech that can convert a code-mixed sentence into high-quality speech with natural pronunciation. Specifically, we propose Language Shift Embedding (LSE) not only to train transliterated words but also to control pronunciation. This means that native pronunciation can be obtained without prior knowledge of the characters of the target language. Moreover, we leverage Conditional Instance Normalization (CIN) to improve pronunciation while preserving the speaker. The experimental results show that FACTSpeech generates high-quality native speech sounds even from the sentences of transliterated form.
